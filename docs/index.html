10nalytics Hackathon Submission 2024

This report begins by exploring the relationship between vaccination coverage and child mortality in Africa. The analysis reveals a significant negative correlation between vaccination rates and mortality, with a correlation coefficient of -0.25 at a significance level of 0.01 (p = 0.00). These findings demonstrate the role of immunization in reducing under-five (U5) and under-fifteen (U15) mortality rates, underscoring the need for robust vaccination campaigns to save lives and achieve Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-Being). The study also identifies distinct age-based trends in mortality causes, which can inform tailored healthcare interventions. Normalized comparisons across countries reveal disparities in vaccination coverage and mortality rates, with Ghana and Gambia excelling in immunization, while Nigeria, Equatorial Guinea, and Chad face significant challenges. Despite these disparities, the consistent decline in U5 mortality rates since 2000 across Africa indicates progress in child survival, aided by interventions such as malaria eradication programs. Malaria's transition from being the leading cause of death to a less prominent one demonstrates the success of concerted global efforts and highlights the potential of similar strategies for other health priorities

To address the issues raised above, policymakers should prioritize comprehensive vaccination programs, especially in countries like Nigeria and Chad with low coverage. This involves partnering with international organizations for funding and equitable vaccine distribution, and employing community engagement strategies to overcome barriers and increase immunization uptake. Improving healthcare infrastructure in regions with high maternal and infant mortality is also essential. Strengthening health systems ensures timely access to vaccines and medical services, supported by investments in training healthcare workers and equipping facilities. Age-specific disease research and interventions should be prioritized to allocate resources efficiently and design tailored public health campaigns. This approach addresses the unique health risks faced by different demographic groups. Building on the success of malaria eradication, stakeholders should replicate evidence-based strategies to combat other leading causes of death, such as lower respiratory infections. Collaboration between governments, NGOs, and private entities is key to sustaining progress in reducing child mortality and improving health outcomes.
